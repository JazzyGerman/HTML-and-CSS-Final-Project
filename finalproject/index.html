Types of Guitars

Acoustic Guitar

The acoustic guitar is wood string instrument with a hollow body and a single hole that strings go over. The vibrations of the strings echo through the body of the guitar to create warm and full sounds. This is a popular instrument for solo artists, country artists, classical guitarists, and more!

Electric Guitar

The electric guitar is a solid body instrument that has magnetic electronics called pick-ups. These send the vibrations of the strings through the pickups into a digital format and then that signal is played through a amplifier. Its common to add additional effects to this signal such as distortion, reverb, or delay. This is a excellent instrument for rock artists, metal bands, and shredders all over the world!

Banjo

While not a classically defined "guitar", the banjo is a popular stringed instrument amoungst folk, bluegrass, and country musicians. With a thin membrane stretched over its body similar to a snare drum, it offers a unique "twangy" sound that no other instrument offers.

Bass Guitar

Typically with 4 strings, the bass guitar is the lowest-pitched string instrument of the guitar family. This guitar is designed to help drive the groove with the drummer. You'll find this guitar is virtually all styles of music!
